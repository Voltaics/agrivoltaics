Summary of Hours and Justification:

Over the course of the semester, I spent approximately 35–40 hours working on the Android deployment and bug fixing process. The biggest portion of this time was dedicated to setting up and configuring the development environment, including resolving Gradle build issues, managing dependencies, and ensuring compatibility with Android SDK. These setup tasks required me to fix multiple configuration errors before achieving a stable build.

I also spent a large amount of time debugging both build-time and runtime issues. This included fixing crashes, resolving class and dependency errors, and addressing platform-specific bugs that only appeared on Android devices. Many of these problems require iterative testing, analyzing error logs, and researching solutions to identify and resolve root causes effectively.

I dedicated time to testing and preparing the application for deployment. This involved running the app on emulators and physical devices, verifying functionality, and generating signed builds for release on Google Play Console. Overall, the hours reflect the iterative nature of deployment, where debugging, testing, and configuration adjustments are closely interconnected and time intensive.
